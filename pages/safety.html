NYC Safety Tips

Essential safety guidelines for residents and visitors in New York City

  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist spots.
  • Keep personal belongings secure and avoid displaying valuables.
  • Be cautious when using ATMs, particularly at night.
  • Trust your instincts and leave areas that feel unsafe.
  • Use well-lit streets and populated routes after dark.
  • Always wear seatbelts when riding in cars or taxis.
  • Follow pedestrian signals and use crosswalks.
  • Be alert around cyclists and delivery bikes.
  • Avoid distractions such as mobile phone use while crossing streets.
  • Use licensed taxis, MTA services, or trusted ride-sharing apps.
  • Keep a digital copy of your passport and ID on your phone.
  • Beware of pickpockets in busy areas like Times Square and subway stations.
  • Do not accept unsolicited offers for tours or tickets.
  • Avoid empty subway cars late at night.
  • Inform someone you trust about your travel plans.
  • Call 911 for police, fire, or medical emergencies.
  • Call 311 for non-emergency NYC services and information.
  • Know the location of the nearest hospital or police precinct.
  • Follow instructions from local authorities during emergencies.
  • Download official city or weather alert apps if staying long-term.
  • Smoking is prohibited in many public spaces, including parks and restaurants.
  • Alcohol consumption is restricted in public areas.
  • Respect personal space and avoid aggressive behavior.
  • Follow posted rules in public parks, museums, and attractions.
  • Photography may be restricted in certain government buildings.
