Safety Tips

Stay safe with these essential guidelines for citizens and tourists

  • Always be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Keep valuables secure and out of sight.
  • Avoid displaying expensive items in public.
  • Travel in groups when possible, especially at night.
  • Use licensed taxis or trusted ride-sharing services.
  • Wear seatbelts at all times in vehicles.
  • Helmet is mandatory for two-wheeler riders and passengers.
  • Obey traffic signals and speed limits.
  • Avoid using mobile phones while driving.
  • Do not drink and drive.
  • Keep photocopies of passport and visa separately.
  • Beware of pickpockets in tourist spots and public transport.
  • Only use authorized guides.
  • Avoid isolated areas after dark.
  • Share your itinerary with family or friends.
  • Save emergency numbers in your phone.
  • Know the location of nearest police station and hospital.
  • Keep a small first-aid kit handy.
  • Learn basic local phrases for help.
  • Register with your embassy if staying long-term.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly at religious places.
  • Ask permission before photographing people.
  • Remove shoes when entering homes or temples.
  • Use right hand for eating and giving/receiving items.
